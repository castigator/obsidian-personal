## The question: does flipping to 74 lock the money until 74?

**No.** The "retirement date" on a pension plan is a planning/admin marker, not an access gate.

- **Access** is governed by the Normal Minimum Pension Age — currently **55, rising to 57 from 6 April 2028** —
  not by the plan's selected retirement date. Rupert is turning **60**, already above NMPA, so he can take or
  transfer this whenever. A retirement date of 74 does **not** push his earliest-access date out.
- What the retirement date actually drives: (1) **lifestyling** glide-path, (2) **projections/illustrations**,
  (3) **comms timing** (wake-up packs).

## The lifestyling nuance (important — reverses the generic answer)

Generic guidance is "a later retirement date keeps you in growth assets longer." **For this plan that is reversed**,
because the fund has *already* been de-risked as it approached the 19 Jun 2026 date. Per the letter, if Rupert
defers he **stays in the lower-risk investments** — Aviva won't move him back to growth unless he actively instructs.

Implication for the divorce:
- For the agreed strategy ("don't draw before settlement; leave it sitting"), staying in low-risk is broadly
  **fine, arguably helpful** — low volatility means the ~£14k value stays stable for Form E.
- The cost is low growth on the £14k while parked.

## Recommendation

1. **No need to beat the 19 June deadline** purely to stop the flip to 74 — letting it auto-default to 74 *is*
   the deferral that was already wanted ([[020_Finance/Pensions]], "do not draw before settlement").
2. The genuinely valuable action from calling Aviva (0345 366 1643) is **requesting formal CETVs for all three
   Aviva pensions** (Midel, Objective IT, Pearson/FT) for Form E — not preserving any particular retirement date.
3. The £14,132.62 fund value ≈ the Midel CETV for Form E — now confirmed in writing.
4. Lifestyling/fund-choice is an IFA question if growth-vs-stability on this pot ever matters; ties into the
   pension-offsetting strategy in [[Clean Break]] / [[IFAs]].
