Background

  1. We are married and divorce proceedings are under way (case no. 1780-7415-8911-6303). We intend in due course to record our overall financial settlement in a consent order.
  2. During our marriage we together incurred debt on three credit cards. Although each card is held in an individual name, we have always treated these balances as our joint responsibility, in equal shares:
    CardAccount holderApprox. balance (July 2026, excluding current-month interest)
    Barclaycard ending 5002Heather Thomas£7,283
    Halifax credit card ending 0664Heather Thomas£7,222 (combined)
    Barclaycard ending 1005Ann House, Heather's mother

What we have agreed

  1. Rupert takes sole responsibility for the balance on the Barclaycard ending 5002 (approx. £7,283). He intends to repay it in full in a single payment, funded by borrowing in his own name.
  2. Heather takes sole responsibility for the balances on the Halifax card ending 0664 and the Barclaycard ending 1005 (approx. £7,222 combined), to repay or refinance as she sees fit.
  3. This is a near-equal division and we each accept it as full settlement of our respective half share of the joint card debt. Once each of us has taken on our side as above, neither of us will seek any contribution from the other in respect of these balances, and neither of us will treat the other's payment as anything other than the discharge of that person's own share (in particular, not as a gift, loan, or maintenance).
  4. We will each keep evidence of the balances at the date of this agreement and of the payments made, and we ask that this agreement be recorded as a recital in our consent order.
  5. This agreement deals only with the credit card balances listed above. All other financial matters between us — including the family home, pensions, and sums owed to family members — remain to be agreed and will be dealt with separately in the consent order.
