# Contribution rebalance + four-point message to Heather

**Date:** 15 Jul 2026
**Question:** Rupert wants to message Heather proposing four changes: (1) split the shared credit
cards, (2) stop paying for the cats, (3) rebalance the monthly 80/20 to account for his own housing
costs, (4) flag the upcoming RBS approach. Verify his "86%/14%" and "~£128 extra" workings, build
the formula, draft the message.

**Sources reviewed:** finance repo `notes/monthly-contributions.md` + `accounts/shared-debt.md` +
`notes/paying-off-50pc-shared-debt.md` (all as of 10 Jul 2026); CLAUDE.md Westgate costs.

## Verifying the remembered figures

- **"86% / 14%":** counting Rupert's FULL Westgate outgoings (~£1,410/mo mid-range) in the pot:
  Rupert (2,701.61 + 1,410) / (3,360.76 + 1,410) = **86.2%**, Heather 13.8%. ✓ matches.
- **"~£128 extra":** counting HALF of Rupert's core housing only (rent £1,000 + council tax £152
  = £1,152; half = **£576**, assuming Mandy covers the other half):
  - Pot = 3,360.76 + 576 = £3,936.76 → Rupert's 80% target = £3,149.41
  - Rupert actually bears 2,701.61 + 576 = £3,277.61 → **overpaying by £128.20/mo** ✓ matches.
  - Equivalently: Heather's 20% target = £787.35 vs £659.15 now = **+£128.20/mo from her side**.

## The formula

> Shared pot = all Pebmarsh commitments + half of Rupert's core housing (rent + council tax only,
> other half attributed to Mandy). Split the pot 80/20. Rupert's transfer = 80% of pot − £576.

Deliberately conservative: it counts only half his housing, and only rent + council tax (not
utilities/broadband/etc.), so it's hard to argue against as over-reaching.

## Interaction with the other proposals (stacking)

The £128.20 is computed on the CURRENT pot. If the cats (£65 → Heather's own) and the card split
(£454.65 servicing drops out — each services their own financing) both happen first:

- Pot becomes 1,871.42 + 719.69 + 250 + 576 = £3,417.11 → Rupert 80% = £2,733.69
- Rupert bears 1,497.14 + 575.75 + 200 + 576 = £2,848.89 → overpay = **£115.20/mo**

So: **£128/mo** if rebalance applied to today's setup; **~£115/mo** in the end-state after cards
and cats are resolved. Quote "~£115–130 depending on sequencing" or pick one basis.

## Card split (point 1)

from Rupert's side; cash routes only (0% money-transfer card ~£295 fee, or personal loan £7,500

## Devil's advocate (flag, not a blocker)

Combined effect on Heather if everything lands: +£128 rebalance + £65 cats + servicing her own
~£7,400 card half. On her ~£1,471/mo net income that is a material squeeze. Risks:
1. **Maintenance ammunition** — her solicitor can present the squeeze as evidence she cannot cope
   without spousal maintenance, cutting against the clean-break goal. The 80% subsidy is currently
   Rupert's best "no maintenance needed — look how generous the interim was" exhibit.
2. **Goodwill** — cooperation (valuations access, trees, drain claim, consent order) has been good;
   four asks at once may read as a pincer. Sequencing/softening matters.
3. Counterpoint: she proposed paying surplus toward a credit card herself (6 Jul), so the debt
   conversation is already open, and the rebalance formula is demonstrably conservative.
