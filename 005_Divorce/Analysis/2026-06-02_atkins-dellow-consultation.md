## The process — as Alan walked it through

This was one flowing walk-through of the whole process (notes images 1→4 are sequential, not separate topics).

### Divorce timeline backbone (no-fault, E&W)

1. **Application** (Rupert) → court **Review** → **Issue**
2. → **Acknowledgement of receipt** (by Heather — the respondent)
3. **20-week period** — the statutory wait. **You use this window to negotiate and draft the consent order in parallel.** ("You're supposed to spend 20 weeks to get that straight.")
4. → **Conditional Order** — after the 20 weeks a judge reviews **remotely** and grants it. It comes back **bearing a date.**
5. That date is the pivot: **the earliest the Final Order / final financial remedy can be made is 6 weeks + 1 day after the Conditional Order.**
6. The **Financial Remedy Order is conditional** on the above sequence.

> ✅ **Nomenclature / sequencing — VERIFIED 2 Jun** (see `2026-06-02_divorce-process-flow-validated.md`): you can *negotiate/draft* the consent order during the 20 weeks, but the court **cannot make it (and you can't submit the D81) until the Conditional Order is pronounced**; the order **takes effect on the Final Order**. So yes — the consent order is gated behind the Conditional Order. Correct terms: Conditional Order (= decree nisi), Final Order (= decree absolute), Form A (filed "for dismissal purposes only" to deliver the clean break), D81 = Statement of Information. Timing: statutory min ~26 wks, real-world avg ~44 wks.

### The financial side — D81 / consent order

- **D81 = Statement of Information** (15/16-page form) filed with the consent order.
- It's a **high-level snapshot + projection** — **bald figures, no evidence/justification required at this stage** (because there's no dispute).
- Has **explanatory boxes** where Alan can set out *why* certain figures are what they are.
- The court tests the whole thing for **FAIRNESS**.
- Atkins Dellow charge a **fixed fee to draft** the consent order (the £1,600+VAT).

- This implies she could potentially be part of **the same single-solicitor flow** — Alan drafts, Heather agrees.
- **Rupert to follow up on this.** Cheapest path by far.
- *Caveat to test with Alan:* a firm can't *act for both* parties (conflict). Normal model = Alan acts for Rupert, drafts the order, Heather agrees without separate representation. Risk: an unadvised Heather (or her existing solicitor) could later claim she didn't understand what she signed. Pin down how this is handled.

### Family Mediation Service — get them in early

- Alan's steer: **"it's never too early"** to involve mediation.
- Mediation produces a **Memorandum of Understanding**.
- **Cost ≈ ⅓ of the solicitor route.**
- Subtle but important point: the formal court procedure (Form A → hearings → Form E → ES1/ES2) is the **contested** process — **it doesn't really apply to Rupert** since this isn't a dispute. Mediation is **voluntary, sits outside the court process**, and just helps work through to agreement.

### The contested route (what Rupert is steering AWAY from)

Only relevant as the fallback if agreement fails:

- Contested → **Form A** to start.
- **First Appointment hearing at 16/20 weeks** + a **Directions Order**.
- → **Form E** — full financial disclosure, ~**30 pages**, **non-voluntary/compulsory**.
- **ES1 & ES2 (Efficiency Statements 1 & 2) are derivatives of Form E** (summaries), not standalone.
- **Exchange → review & challenge** (~2 wks) → typically **3+ hearings → delays**.
- The **First Appointment can be moved straight to an FDR** (Financial Dispute Resolution) to save time.
- **Form H** (costs estimate) filed **at every step**.
- Overall timing he mentioned: ~**3–4 weeks** and ~**6–7 months** (which maps to which — TODO confirm; likely 3–4 wks for order approval once submitted, 6–7 months for the whole process).
