Solicitor Cost Estimate — Consent Order Process

Prepared 29 May 2026 | For reference at solicitor consultations

Key Variables

Rupert's case is moderately complex — property with subsidence, multiple pensions, joint debt, income disparity. But not high-conflict: both employed adults with adult children, no child arrangements needed, goal is a negotiated consent order.

Stages and Estimated Hours

1. Initial consultation & advice (1–2 hours)

  • Review briefing pack and financials
  • Advise on likely outcomes, strategy, pension approach
  • Discuss whether PODE is needed
  • Cost at £402/hr: £402–£804

2. Financial disclosure / Form E preparation (3–6 hours)

  • Each party completes Form E
  • Solicitor reviews, advises, chases missing info
  • Reviewing Heather's Form E
  • Querying valuations, pension CETVs, debt documentation
  • Cost: £1,206–£2,412

3. Negotiation and correspondence (5–25+ hours)

This is the biggest cost driver. Depends on how cooperative Heather's side is.

  • Solicitor-to-solicitor correspondence
  • Advising on offers and counter-offers
  • Spousal maintenance arguments
  • Pension offsetting vs sharing analysis
  • Property sale timing, joint debt allocation

4. Pension work (2–5 hours)

  • Obtaining formal CETVs (Rupert has ~6 pensions, Heather at least 1 DB)
  • If a PODE is instructed: £1,500–£5,000 for the expert's fee (separate)
  • Solicitor time reviewing PODE report, drafting pension sharing annex
  • Solicitor cost: £804–£2,010

5. Drafting consent order & D81 (2–4 hours)

  • Drafting the consent order, Statement of Information (Form D81), pension sharing annex
  • Cost: £804–£1,608

6. Court submission and follow-up (1–2 hours)

  • Filing with the court (£60 court fee)
  • Responding to any queries from the judge
  • Cost: £402–£804 + £60 court fee

  • Form A to First Appointment: +£5,000–£10,000

This is why negotiating a consent order is strongly preferable.

Cost-Saving Strategies

  1. Do your own legwork — briefing pack, pension tracing, financial spreadsheet all reduce solicitor hours
  2. Get CETVs before instructing — avoids chasing time
  3. Consider mediation — £130–£400/hr per person, can resolve disputes faster
  4. Fixed-fee consent order drafting — if agreement is reached via mediation, drafting can be £1,500–£3,000
  5. Choose your battles — every point of contention adds hours

Notes

  • All figures include VAT at 20% unless stated
  • Court fee for consent order: £60
  • Court fee for divorce application: £612 (separate)
  • PODE fees are a disbursement, not part of solicitor's bill
  • Solicitor may require money on account (typically £2,000–£5,000 upfront)
  • Both parties pay their own solicitor
  • This is research, not legal advice
