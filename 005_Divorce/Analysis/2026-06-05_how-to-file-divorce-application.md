## How to File the Divorce Application — What's Actually Required

**Date:** 5 June 2026 · **Basis:** gov.uk divorce service + Form D8, verified against the validated process flow.

> Not legal advice. This is the mechanics of *filing the divorce application* — the opening step that starts the  
> 20-week clock. It is **separate from, and far simpler than, the financial settlement.** See  
> [[2026-06-02_divorce-process-flow-validated]] and [[2026-06-03_sole-vs-joint-and-solicitor-models]].

---

### The Headline

Filing the divorce application is **essentially a press-button exercise.** It needs **no financial information,  
no consent order, and no reason/grounds** (it's no-fault). The only physical dependency is the **marriage  
certificate**. Everything financial happens *later*, during the 20 weeks the application sets running.

This is **step one**, not a later step — filing starts the **20-week statutory clock**, and the financial work  
(gather numbers → mediation → draft consent order) runs **concurrently, inside** that window. So there is real  
value in filing **early**: the clock is on the critical path to the Final Order, and every week unfiled is a week  
of dual-housing cost (~£4k/month) not being worked off. See [[project_file_application_first]].

---

### What You Need to File (the Whole list)

**Information:**
- **Both full names**, exactly as on the marriage certificate (Rupert Thomas / Heather Thomas).
- **Both addresses** — Rupert's (19 Westgate Street, Long Melford) and **Heather's current address** (Maple Tree  
  House, 1 Kingsmead, Pebmarsh) — the court needs hers to deliver/serve documents.
- **Marriage date** — 22 August 1998.
- Optionally **Heather's email address** (enables online service — faster).
- **Proof of name change** — only if a name changed since the marriage. **N/A** here.

**Document (the only one):**
- **Original marriage certificate or a certified copy**, uploaded as an image with **all four corners visible**.
  - ⚠️ Rupert's digital copy **does not show all four corners** → order a **fresh certified copy** (~£11 from the  
    GRO, gro.gov.uk). Easy; this is the one thing to obtain before filing.

**Fee:** **£612**, paid online by card (one fee total — *not* doubled for a joint application). Fee help via form  
**EX160** if eligible (on a joint application, both apply for help separately).

---

### What You Do NOT Need at Filing (the reassurance)

- ❌ **No financial information** — no asset values, no pension CETVs, no house valuation, no debt figures.
- ❌ **No consent order** — it doesn't exist yet; it's drafted later and lodged after the Conditional Order.
- ❌ **No reason / grounds / evidence** — no-fault: you simply confirm the marriage has **irretrievably broken  
  down**. Nothing to prove, nobody to blame.

### The One Nuance — the Financial-order Tick Box

The application includes a box stating you **intend to apply for financial orders.** **Tick it.** It asks for  
**no figures and no detail** — it's just a placeholder that keeps your financial claims open so the consent order  
can be lodged later (it seeds the "Form A"). Forgetting to tick it is the classic own-goal. **Tick it.**

---

### Sole Vs Joint — the Filing Mechanics

| | **Sole** (Rupert applies) | **Joint** (both apply) |
|---|---|---|
| How it's filed | Rupert completes + submits **alone, immediately** | Same single form, but **goes to Heather to confirm her half online** before it's issued |
| Speed to start the clock | **Today**, no dependency on Heather | Only as fast as Heather completes her part |
| Heather's involvement | She's served, returns one **Acknowledgement of Service** later (concurrent) | She must act at filing **and** at each later milestone |
| Tone | Still fully **non-adversarial** under no-fault (no blame, she loses nothing) | Reads as collaborative |

Both cost the same £612, take the same time from issue, and give the same outcome. The choice is **control/speed  
vs tone** — decided by reading how readily Heather will engage with the admin. Full analysis:  
[[2026-06-03_sole-vs-joint-and-solicitor-models]].

---

### Where to File

Online: **gov.uk/apply-for-divorce** (the standard route; needs a card and an email). Or by post using **Form D8**.
