## The formula

> Pot = Pebmarsh core bills + 50% of Westgate core bills. Split 80/20.
> Rupert pays 80% of pot − his Westgate half (£2,554.44 − £644.54 net new transfer basis);
> Heather pays 20% = £638.61, i.e. **+£128.91/mo vs her current £509.70**.

Conservative: only half his housing counted, and only core bills (no broadband/TV licence etc.).

## Devil's advocate (flag, not a blocker)

Combined effect on Heather if everything lands: +£128 rebalance + £65 cats + servicing her own
~£7,400 card half. On her ~£1,471/mo net income that is a material squeeze. Risks:
1. **Maintenance ammunition** — her solicitor can present the squeeze as evidence she cannot cope
   without spousal maintenance, cutting against the clean-break goal. The 80% subsidy is currently
   Rupert's best "no maintenance needed — look how generous the interim was" exhibit.
2. **Goodwill** — cooperation (valuations access, trees, drain claim, consent order) has been good;
   four asks at once may read as a pincer. Sequencing/softening matters.
3. Counterpoint: she proposed paying surplus toward a credit card herself (6 Jul), so the debt
   conversation is already open, and the rebalance formula is demonstrably conservative.
