## 6. Assessment

### Is £550,000 (as-is, with subsidence) realistic?

**Yes — well supported by evidence.**

- A direct neighbour on Kings Mead has just sold STC at OIEO £550,000. That property needs modernisation but (presumably) does not have active subsidence. A subsidence-affected property should arguably be lower.
- Detached houses in Pebmarsh have sold from £485,000-£835,000 recently. £550,000 sits in the lower-middle of the range, appropriate for a property with an active subsidence claim and ongoing monitoring.
- In Great Maplestead, detached houses are achieving £540,000-£765,000.
- The £550,000 figure may actually be slightly generous given the subsidence — buyers must accept an ongoing insurance claim, potential delays, and stigma. A 10-15% discount from unaffected value is typical for subsidence properties, which from a £650k base would suggest ~£550-585k.

### Is £650,000 (post-repair, no subsidence) realistic?

**Yes — conservative if anything.**

- Oakdene on Oak Road sold for £812,000 (Sep 2025).
- Great Lengths on The Street sold for £747,500 (May 2022).
- Wheatlands on Water Lane sold for £800,000 (Jan 2024).
- In Great Maplestead, several detached houses have sold in the £630,000-£765,000 range.
- The Kings Mead neighbour at £550,000 needs modernisation — a fully repaired and modernised Maple Tree House could reasonably exceed £650,000.
- Current listings show 4-bed detached houses being marketed at £750,000-£795,000 in Pebmarsh.

**Summary:** The Sep 2025 agent valuations of £550k (as-is) and £650k (post-repair) appear sound and possibly conservative, particularly the post-repair figure. The market evidence supports a post-repair value of £650,000-£700,000, and the as-is figure of £550,000 is in line with the Kings Mead STC sale, though that property lacks subsidence issues.

### Key risk factors

- **Subsidence stigma:** Even after repair, some buyers will avoid former subsidence properties or demand a discount. This could reduce the achievable price by 5-10% vs a never-affected property.
- **Cash settlement option:** If GHG offers a cash settlement, the net equity calculation changes. The repair cost vs cash settlement gap matters.
- **Time cost:** Each month of delay costs ~£4,000 in dual housing. A property with active subsidence monitoring will sell more slowly and likely at the lower end of the range.
- **Limited comparable data on Kings Mead:** No sales since 2007 until this current STC sale — the development is very stable with long-term residents, which limits price discovery.
