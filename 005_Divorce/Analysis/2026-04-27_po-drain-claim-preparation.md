## Potential pushback and how to handle it

### "This is part of the subsidence claim"
**Response:** GHG's own letter explicitly says the drain damage is NOT implicated in the subsidence. The drains are damaged by tree root physical ingress. The subsidence is caused by root-induced clay shrinkage desiccation. Different mechanism, different location, different claim.

### "You need to claim on your drain insurance (HomeServe)"
**Response:** The HomeServe policy is a plumbing and drainage maintenance policy with a £4,000 per claim limit. The repair cost is £9,360. The PO buildings insurance covers accidental damage to underground services — this is the appropriate policy. GHG (your appointed loss adjuster) specifically recommended claiming on the home insurance.

### "The damage was pre-existing"
**Response:** The damage was discovered on 6 March 2026 during a CCTV survey. There was no prior knowledge of the drain damage. Root ingress is a gradual process — the damage developed over time while the policy was in force.

### "The excess should be £1,000 (subsidence)"
**Response:** This is not a subsidence claim. It is accidental damage to underground services caused by tree root ingress. The subsidence excess applies to subsidence claims only. The standard buildings excess of £400 should apply.

### "You should have notified us sooner"
**Response:** The damage was discovered 6 March 2026 as part of the subsidence investigation. GHG Solutions (your appointed loss adjuster) was aware immediately and included the findings in their April 2026 report. GHG recommended submitting a separate claim, which we are now doing promptly.
