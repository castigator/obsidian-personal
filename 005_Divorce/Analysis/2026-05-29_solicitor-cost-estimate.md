# Solicitor Cost Estimate — Consent Order Process

**Date:** 29 May 2026
**Question:** What are realistic solicitor costs for Rupert's consent order, broken down by stage?

---

## Key Variables

Rupert's case is **moderately complex** — not simple (there's a property with subsidence, multiple pensions, joint debt, income disparity) but not high-conflict either. Both parties are employed adults with adult children. No child arrangements needed. The goal is a negotiated consent order, not contested proceedings.

**Francesca Easter's rate:** £335/hr + VAT = **£402/hr inc VAT**

---

## Stages and Estimated Hours

### 1. Initial consultation & advice (1–2 hours)
- Review briefing pack and financials
- Advise on likely outcomes, strategy, and pension approach
- Discuss whether PODE (pension expert) is needed
- **Cost at £402/hr: £402–£804**

### 2. Financial disclosure / Form E preparation (3–6 hours)
- Each party completes Form E (the standard financial disclosure form)
- Solicitor reviews, advises on what to include, chases missing info
- Reviewing Heather's Form E when received
- Querying valuations, pension CETVs, debt documentation
- **Cost: £1,206–£2,412**

### 3. Negotiation and correspondence (5–15 hours)
This is the **widest range** and the biggest cost driver. Depends entirely on how cooperative Heather's side is.
- Solicitor-to-solicitor correspondence (letters, emails, calls)
- Advising on offers and counter-offers
- Spousal maintenance arguments (likely to be raised given income gap)
- Pension offsetting vs. pension sharing analysis
- Property sale timing discussions
- Joint debt allocation
- **Agreed / light negotiation: 5–8 hours → £2,010–£3,216**
- **Moderate negotiation: 8–15 hours → £3,216–£6,030**
- **If it gets difficult: 15–25+ hours → £6,030–£10,050+**

### 4. Pension work (2–5 hours)
- Obtaining formal CETVs (Rupert has ~6 pensions, Heather has at least 1 DB)
- If a PODE (Pension on Divorce Expert) is instructed: £1,500–£3,000 for the expert's fee (separate from solicitor time)
- Solicitor time reviewing PODE report, drafting pension sharing annex
- Liaising with pension administrators
- **Solicitor cost: £804–£2,010**
- **PODE fee (if needed): £1,500–£3,000 (shared or one party pays)**

### 5. Drafting the consent order & D81 (2–4 hours)
- Drafting the consent order itself
- Preparing the Statement of Information (Form D81)
- Pension sharing annex if applicable
- Both parties review and sign
- **Cost: £804–£1,608**

### 6. Court submission and follow-up (1–2 hours)
- Filing with the court (£60 court fee)
- Responding to any queries from the judge
- Chasing if delayed
- **Cost: £402–£804 + £60 court fee**

### If it goes to court (Financial Remedy proceedings)
If agreement can't be reached and formal court proceedings are needed:
- Form A to First Appointment: +£5,000–£10,000
- First Appointment to FDR hearing: +£10,000–£15,000
- FDR to Final Hearing: +£10,000–£20,000
- **Total contested: £25,000–£50,000+**

This is why negotiating a consent order is so strongly preferable.

---

## What "20–40 hours" Means

The 20–40 hour range covers the **moderate scenario** — a negotiated consent order where:
- Both sides engage in disclosure
- There's meaningful but not hostile negotiation
- Pensions need expert analysis
- The order gets drafted, signed, and sealed

It does NOT include contested court proceedings. If Heather's solicitor is reasonable and the income/pension gap can be bridged with sensible offsetting, 20–30 hours is realistic. If maintenance becomes a sticking point, it pushes toward 35–40+.

---

## Cost-Saving Strategies

1. **Do your own legwork** — Rupert is already doing this well (briefing pack, pension tracing, financial spreadsheet). Every hour the solicitor doesn't spend gathering info saves £402.
2. **Get CETVs before instructing** — having all pension values ready avoids chasing time.
3. **Consider mediation** — a mediator costs £100–£300/hr (split between parties) and can resolve disputes faster than solicitor-to-solicitor letters.
4. **Fixed-fee consent order drafting** — if you reach agreement via mediation, the actual drafting can be done for £1,500–£3,000 by a specialist service. But this only works if the agreement is already settled.
5. **Choose your battles** — every point of contention adds hours. Prioritise what matters (pension split, clean break) and concede on smaller items.
