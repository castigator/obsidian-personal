## The Trees

### Maple (Acer spp.)
- **Location:** 4.5m from the extension (right side)
- **Root findings:** Roots confirmed at foundation level in TP2/BH2 — roots to 5mm diameter at 0.6m depth, abundant starch (actively growing)
- **Foundations at this location:** 0.6m deep, 300mm concrete (very shallow)
- **Damage caused:** Desiccating clay beneath extension foundations — likely primary cause of utility room cracking (25mm crack showing 10.21mm seasonal closure)

### Willow (Salix spp.)
- **Location:** 12.5m from front right of property
- **Root findings:** Roots confirmed at foundation level in TP1/BH1 — roots at 1.6m depth, moderate starch (actively growing)
- **Foundations at this location:** 1.45m deep, 250mm concrete
- **Damage caused:** Desiccating clay beneath front foundations — likely primary cause of front room cracking (3mm crack)
- **Note:** Willows are aggressive water-seekers — roots can extend 40m+. At 12.5m from the house this is well within range. Likely the larger and more established of the two trees.

### Why removal is required

GHG's confirmed diagnosis: **root-induced clay shrinkage**. The trees' roots are drawing moisture from the clay soil beneath the foundations, causing it to shrink and the foundations to subside. Removal stops the cause. The clay then rehydrates over subsequent months, monitored until stable, then structural repairs can proceed.
