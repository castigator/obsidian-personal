7. Spousal Maintenance — Risk Assessment

Given the income gap (£6,109 vs £1,471) and 27 years of marriage, Heather's solicitor is likely to raise spousal maintenance. Rupert's position:

Arguments for clean break

  • Heather is employed full-time as an office manager and has earning capacity
  • All children are adults — no childcare constraints
  • Courts now favour fixed-term orders over open-ended joint lives orders
  • A lump sum from equity can bridge any short-term income gap
  • As of May 2026, Heather is applying for single occupancy council tax discount (25% reduction)

Risks

  • 27-year marriage is long — courts give more weight to established living standards
  • The income disparity is significant (4:1 ratio)
  • If Heather's pension provision is very low, the court may factor retirement needs into maintenance

Affordability modelling is in progress to demonstrate that Heather can be financially independent post-settlement with her equity share. Preliminary figures suggest her income shortfall against reasonable living costs could be bridged by equity alone for 20+ years, but this needs further work.
